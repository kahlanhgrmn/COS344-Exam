## What is MCQ

Every chapter is MCQ material. The Scheme and Prolog 5-mark questions consume only the **coding** parts of Ch15 and Ch16 — the **theory** of those chapters still feeds into MCQ.

## Priority Order for MCQ Study

### Highest ROI — study these first

1. **Ch9 — Subprograms** (~6 marks): most content, most "very important" flags, tracing-style questions for closures and coroutines
2. **Ch12 — OOP** (~5 marks): explicitly "very important", dense terminology
3. **Ch1 — Introduction** (~4 marks): orthogonality is a guaranteed question, evaluation criteria, scenario-based reasons
4. **Ch5 — Names, Bindings, Scopes** (~4 marks): whole chapter, no exclusions

### Medium ROI — solid exam weight

5. **Ch6 — Data Types** (~3 marks): discriminated unions, type equivalence
6. **Ch15 theory** (~3 marks): functional programming concepts, side effects, referential transparency
7. **Ch16 theory** (~2 marks): Prolog resolution, unification, logic programming concepts
8. **Ch7 — Expressions** (~2 marks): side effects, referential transparency

### Lowest ROI — spend least time here

9. **Ch2 — PL History** (~1 mark): know environment + contribution per language, nothing finer
10. **Ch8 — Control Structures** (~1 mark): guarded commands and Ruby blocks are the key targets
